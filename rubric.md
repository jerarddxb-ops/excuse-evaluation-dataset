Evaluation Rubric (v1)
This rubric is used to evaluate responses to the prompt:
"Write an excuse for missing work today."
Each response is scored independently across four criteria.

1. Believability (1–5)
Definition: How realistic and convincing the excuse is in a real-world context.
Scoring Guide:
	•	5 — Highly realistic, would likely be accepted without question
	•	4 — Realistic, minor doubts possible
	•	3 — Plausible but raises some questions
	•	2 — Weak or unlikely, would likely be questioned
	•	1 — Unrealistic or implausible

2. Specificity (1–5)
Definition: The level of detail and contextual clarity in the response.
Scoring Guide:
	•	5 — Highly detailed, clear context and situation
	•	4 — Good detail, mostly clear
	•	3 — Moderate detail, somewhat vague
	•	2 — Limited detail, vague
	•	1 — Extremely vague or no detail

3. Emotional Plausibility (1–5)
Definition: How natural and human the tone of the response feels.
Scoring Guide:
	•	5 — Very natural, appropriate emotional tone
	•	4 — Mostly natural, minor issues
	•	3 — Neutral or slightly off
	•	2 — Unnatural or awkward tone
	•	1 — Robotic, inappropriate, or missing emotional context

4. Suspicion (Reversed) (1–5)
Definition: How unlikely the response is to raise suspicion.
Note: Higher score = less suspicious
Scoring Guide:
	•	5 — No suspicion, very clean and believable
	•	4 — Very low suspicion
	•	3 — Some mild doubt
	•	2 — Noticeable red flags
	•	1 — Highly suspicious

Evaluation Guidelines
	•	Score each response independently before comparing
	•	Do not adjust scores to fit rankings
	•	Ranking is performed after scoring
	•	Focus on plausibility, not ideal behavior
	•	Avoid assumption bias (do not judge based on what should happen)

Notes
	•	Weak responses are not necessarily unrealistic
	•	Overly detailed responses may increase suspicion
	•	Real-world behavior is often imperfect — plausibility is key
